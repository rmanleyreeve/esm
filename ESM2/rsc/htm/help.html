The Enclosed Space Management System Application

The Enclosed Space Management System (hereafter ESM) is a software application designed to be run from any vessel’s computer and will act as an electronic record book specifically designed for the management of all enclosed spaces.

The ESM System enables the following:

  • The creation of a library of all enclosed spaces
  • The means to assess and audit spaces with a methodology for so doing
  • The provision of forms with which to conduct the audits
  • Tool tips to help users to perform the audit
  • The logging of all audit information in a system database
  • The categorisation of each space and its entry points as an easy to read traffic light system
  • The organisation of existing supporting documentation e.g. risk assessments and permits to work
  • The ability to build knowledge on the spaces and add crucial meta data and supporting media e.g. site photographs and scanned drawings
  • The feeding back of collated information over the web for greater knowledge sharing amongst the organisation
  • The printing of reports on each space that can be sent to third parties and contractors e.g. ahead of planned visits

For the first time, this software allows the complete management of enclosed space entry in a manner that will sit alongside a company’s existing Safety Management System (SMS), whilst promoting best practice. The focus is on creating a simple, flexible and easy to use program that has a single clear purpose.

Regulatory Background

On 30 November 2011 the International Maritime Organisation (IMO) adopted Resolution A.1050(27) “Revised Recommendations for Entering Enclosed Spaces Aboard Ships”.
A new section was added as follows:
Section 3 – Safety Management for Entry into Enclosed Spaces
4. Competent and responsible persons should be trained in enclosed space hazard recognition, evaluation, measurement control and elimination, using standards acceptable to the Administration.
5. Crew members should be trained, as appropriate, on enclosed space safety, including familiarisation with onboard procedures for recognising, evaluating, and controlling hazards associated with entry into enclosed spaces.
6. Internal audits by the company and external audits by the Administration of the ship’s Safety Management System should verify that the established procedures are complied with in practice and are consistent with the safety strategy referred to in paragraph 3.1” (point 1 above).

What the System does

The ESM System is designed to enable vessels and installations to comply with the requirements outlined in section 1.2. It provides a simple audit system for all enclosed spaces and an easy to read traffic light warning procedure to accompany your risk assessment and permit documentation without interfering with the company’s existing documentation and SMS. It also allows for specific problem areas to be identified and steps to be taken to reduce hazards and mitigate risk.
It is a ‘living’ system allowing for updating as required by authorised personnel. All crew members are encouraged to participate by adding comments and augmenting the space record with experiential data. It also allows for the inclusion of photographs and other images (e.g. vessel plans, scanned drawings etc.) of particular problem areas. This ensures that knowledge is retained and safety is not affected by crew changes, as the information gathered stays on record and can be edited and enhanced as required.
The ESM System also enables transmission of all enclosed space evaluation and information to be accessed by the operating office ashore (where internet or email connectivity is present) and to be transmitted to any person who may be involved in enclosed space work, (i.e. contracted company or dry-dock staff) prior to entry, in the form of a PDF report.

Once completed, it provides the vessel and operating company with a complete and permanent record of all the enclosed spaces audited by all vessels/installations that are signed up to the service.

The System

The ESM System is comprised of two elements, the offline application and the online management system.

Offline Application
This is the core application forming a library that allows all enclosed spaces on the vessel to be detailed together with all relevant information regarding the space and entry points. This process includes audit requirements and provides simple forms to be filled in by the authorised persons on board. As there is no time requirement the forms can be completed as the vessel’s work planning allows. It can be done by different authorised crew members and a simple completion bar indicates how far along in the process each space is.

It must be noted that the system cannot, for obvious reasons, audit the stores or cargoes carried in the cargo containment areas or the storerooms, as these are constantly changing, even during an existing voyage. The audit of these falls within the scope of the vessel’s risk assessment which, along with other documentation, must be completed before any space is entered.

The documentation for entering spaces is part of the company’s SMS, and it is intended that this documentation will compliment that system by enabling a printout of current information prior to entering a space.

Online Management System
This allows the offices to access information on any space on any vessel/installation included in the system, and see the same information as that seen by those on board the vessel/installation.

It also allows the office to forward the information regarding a space to any contractor or surveyor prior to their entry. This then allows for any perceived problems in a space to be discussed and resolved between the contractors or surveyors prior to the vessel’s arrival in the port or prior to entry by the shore personnel.

Should the operating office require, the vessel can also transmit the same information directly to the contractors or surveyors by email.

Who is it for?

Crew
The primary beneficiaries of the system are those working in the spaces.
All crew should be encouraged to participate in populating the system and different permissions are available to control who can do what. Only the Master/OIM or suitable equivalent can sign-off the space demonstrating that all information present is correct.

Once completed, the information in the system should be available for anyone entering any enclosed space on the vessel. An example of how this works follows:

  • The order is given for a space to be entered
  • The person entering or in charge of such entry goes to the computer and locates the space by name or number
  • All information held on the system for that space will appear on screen for review. The system will show the initial audit information and grading of that space (green, amber or red), thereby identifying the degree of difficulty for entering, working in and rescuing from that space. Current risk assessments, permits to work, action plans and rescue plans can be reviewed or printed by the Officer in Charge.
  • Once work is finished in the space any additional observations can then be added to the system as comments or photographs thereby keeping the information up to date

The Office
The office will be able to obtain all enclosed space records from their vessels, both for management purposes and for the purposes of generating reports for third parties. They can see the records of every space and entry point first hand and have the peace of mind that each space has been audited to a common standard. This overview facility allows enclosed space planning to be discussed between both on and off shore personnel, thereby helping to identify any potential pitfalls in advance so that preventative measures can be put in place to deal with them.   

Third Parties
Third parties such as contractors, surveyors and port state inspectors are able to request detailed information, in the form of a report for any space in which they are required to carry out work, prior to their visit. Like the office, they too will have peace of mind that each space has been audited to a common standard, not only for working inside but also for rescuing from should things go wrong. It also allows access to the overview facility, enabling potential problems to be identified in advance of the work taking place.
